Aircraft, end to end

One artist owns the full path from reference to a flight-ready prefab. No handoffs between five freelancers, no "the rigger left" risk.

EXTERIOR

Airframe that reads at every distance

  • Hand-crafted high poly, clean bakes, no AI-generated assets in the product
  • Trim sheets and shared atlases so ten aircraft do not mean ten material sets
  • Livery masks so variants cost hours, not weeks
COCKPIT

Cockpits built for VR hands

  • Interaction-first layout: every switch, lever and stick modeled to be grabbed
  • Legible instruments at VR resolution, baked AO for depth
  • Separate interior lighting rig for day and dusk flying
RIGGING

Rigged for the flight model, not just the render

  • Control surfaces, gear, props, flaps and canopies rigged with clean pivots and naming your engineers can drive from code
  • Bone-based where it is cheap, blendshape only where it earns its cost
INTEGRATION

Delivered inside your project

  • Works in your Unity project and your version control, daily commits
  • Prefab conventions agreed with your engineering lead up front
  • Collision meshes, damage states and destruction variants on request

Terrain and large open worlds

Big VR worlds die in two ways: they look empty, or they melt the frame budget. The fix is procedural authorship with hand-finished hero areas.

AUTHORING

Gaia Pro and stamp-based sculpting

  • Heightmap stamping for fast, art-directable landmass iteration
  • Biome spawners with rule sets: slope, altitude and mask-driven placement of trees, rocks, props
  • Splat-map texturing with distance blending to kill tiling
SCATTER

Procedural density, artist control

  • Rule-set scatter for forests and fields, density maps painted by hand where it matters
  • Hero areas (airports, towns, landmarks) dressed manually on top of the procedural base
  • Re-runnable: change a rule, regrow the world, keep the hand-placed layer
SCALE

Streaming-friendly world structure

  • Chunked terrain tiles sized with your engineer for streaming and floating origin
  • Impostors and billboards for the far ring, full meshes only near the player
  • Horizon and water treated as cheap backdrops, not simulated geometry
TOOLING

Plays well with editor tooling

  • Comfortable working inside custom Unity editor tools and extending prefab workflows
  • Naming, folder and prefab conventions agreed up front so nothing arrives as a mystery asset
  • Documentation with every delivery, another artist could pick it up cold
